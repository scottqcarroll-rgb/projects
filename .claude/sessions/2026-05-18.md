## What Was Built

### 1. Comprehensive Business Intelligence Extraction
Analyzed a full transcript from Colin Strad (GoSomewhere.com) — a credit card rewards consultant doing $231k revenue in 2025 and on pace to exceed that in 2026. Extracted and organized:

- **Business Model:** Two-offer structure ($875 consulting + $300–$400 trip planning)
- **Revenue Proof:** $1k first month (March 2024) → $13k quit-job month (Nov 2024) → $231k annual (2025) → $89k YTD (Apr 2026)
- **Margin:** ~83% net after ~$40k annual expenses (software, contractors)
- **Startup Cost:** ~$100/month in software subscriptions

### 2. Core Knowledge Inventory
Documented the three layers of expertise required:
- **Earning Points:** Identifying bad card strategies (1% cashback), teaching better earn rates (2–4x)
- **Transferring Points:** Moving points to transfer partners for 2–5¢ per point vs. 1¢ through portals
- **Devaluation Urgency:** Points lose 15–20% value year-over-year — "earn and burn" vs. hoard mentality

**Key insight:** The AMX Business Gold cap trap — earns 4x on ads but capped at $150k/year. After that, drops to 1x. Most business owners don't know this.

### 3. Free Learning Resources Compiled
All free, no paywalls:
- **The Daily Drop** — best free course on points (video + text)
- **The Points Guy, Frequent Miler, One Mile at a Time** — deep-dive blogs
- **Seats.aero** — award search tool with AI assistant (ChatGPT + live airline data)
- **Claude + ChatGPT** — for learning and research

**Insight:** Weekend of reading = more knowledgeable than 90–99% of people in the space.

### 4. Client Acquisition Playbook
100% inbound, zero outbound:
- **LinkedIn:** 1–2 posts/day, 6 days/week — sharing deals, insights, client wins
- **Viral case:** Posted about a deal researched but never booked → 100k impressions with <1k followers
- **Newsletter:** 7k subscribers as a backup channel and affiliate link layer
- **Affiliate revenue:** $400+ per credit card signup added as passive layer

**Ideal client:** Blue-collar business owners (construction, medical, trades) spending $20k–$100k+/month on cards, earning 1% on local bank card, not world travelers yet.

### 5. LinkedIn Post Copy (4 Variations)
Created compelling ad/post options:
- **Option 1 — "Burning Money" hook:** Personal story angle, widest appeal
- **Option 2 — "Cap Trap" education:** Insider knowledge, high engagement, algorithm boost
- **Option 3 — Short punchy version:** For testing, low commitment
- **Option 4 — Personal story angle:** Early-stage credibility builder

**Recommendation:** Start with Option 2 (education post) for maximum organic reach and comment engagement.

## Key Decisions Made

1. **Business Model Choice:** Two-offer structure is superior to one-offer because:
   - $875 consulting captures the high-touch, high-value client
   - $300–$400 trip planning scales with contractors
   - Some clients only need one — pure profit on that offer
   - Both feeds into affiliate link revenue

2. **No Outbound Sales Required:** 100% inbound via LinkedIn content — validates that this market has genuine, urgent demand. Means low customer acquisition cost and all energy goes to delivery.

3. **Pricing Strategy:** Start lower ($400–$500) for early testimonials, raise to $875+ as social proof builds. Same playbook Colin used.

4. **Ideal Client Profile:** Focus on blue-collar business owners, not tech people. Tech people think they already know everything; blue-collar owners spend enormous amounts but are still earning 1% and know nothing. Easy value prop.

5. **Affiliate Links as Revenue Layer:** Don't ignore this. $400 per card signup is material when you have clients trusting your recommendations.

## Critical Unknowns / Next Steps

1. **Domain Registration:** GoSomewhere.WLD.com listed for $11k — need to decide on domain strategy for Plastic Travel. Recommend lowballing domain brokers or finding alternative premium domain.

2. **LinkedIn Sustainability Risk:** Colin is terrified LinkedIn could disappear. His backup: email newsletter (7k subscribers). **Action:** Build email list from day one, not as afterthought.

3. **Scaling Trip Research:** Colin's trip research still doing only ~$6k/month with 35 active trips and two contractors. Path to $30k–$50k/month exists but not yet proven at scale. **Action:** Test paid ads (Meta/Google) only after process is bulletproof.

4. **AI vs. Human Positioning:** Colin noticed Claude can give overly optimistic scenarios ("you can fly to Paris for 100k points in business class" — technically true one day a year). His positioning: human judgment on what's actually bookable. **Action:** Decide whether to lean into human expert angle or build AI-first product.

5. **Personal Travel Experience Requirement:** Colin admits he hasn't been to most places he sends clients to. Doesn't matter if you know the mechanics. **Decision:** No need for extensive personal travel; mechanical knowledge sufficient.
